Every so often, a blue star descends from the cosmos,
fallen from the ink of the goddess’s pen. It plummets
to earth and, rarely, it lands in the heads of those
she deems worthy to join the sages.

Those joined with the Daemon, hear and think
in the tongue of the goddess, logic of the first order. 
Products of the mind are rigorously churned and questioned
until the Daemon is silent. Hearing a sage speak to the Daemon

is like seeing molten silver harden. Before a sage is mature
they must survive the trial. Sad to say, not all touched live.
The inquisitions of the Daemon are taxing on the host,
some take their own life to escape the endless questions.

Others are driven insane. Those who live are often rejected
by their loved ones, their community, or their nation.
They are hated for their undying dedication to truth,
even at the cost of their own well-being. Thus, to be a sage

is the ultimate dedication to Athena. An unending pursuit
of truth, knowledge, and wisdom regardless the cost.
They carry on the legacy of Socrates, carrying on 
the torch of cognition to bring about enlightenment.
